Profile

Research

I work on the mission architectures and starlight-suppression instruments behind direct imaging of potentially habitable worlds — self-consistent starshade concepts (occulter geometry, inner working angle, formation-flying separation), UV-to-NIR spectroscopy trade-offs for biosignature detection, and yield modeling with EXOSIMS/AYO for NASA's Habitable Worlds Observatory. Co-author on HWO and starshade studies indexed in NASA ADS; active in HWO working groups and SGAC.

Engineering

I build the systems that get missions and their data working: satellite architecture modeling, command & data handling, SPENVIS radiation-dose analysis, and link-budget/Δv/power sizing, plus production Python/SQL pipelines, ML models, and workflow automation delivered across consulting, enterprise, and startup settings — including co-founding and leading engineering for a multilingual voice-agent platform.

Habitable Worlds Observatory — exoplanet yield science

Co-author on HWO exoplanet survey-strategy and yield-modeling work (ESYWG / JATIS, under review): detection → orbit determination → spectroscopic characterization workflows, with wavelength, SNR, and detector-noise trade-offs via EXOSIMS/AYO.

Publication 2025

DARES Starshade Astrobiology white paper

Co-author on a broadband, high-throughput exoEarth mission concept (Seager et al., 2025) — led UV-to-near-IR spectroscopy trade-offs for biosignature detection (O₂, O₃, H₂O, CO₂) at <10⁻¹⁰ contrast.

Concept study NASA ASTRA

PETRA — starshade mission concept

Self-consistent occulter design developed for NASA's ASTRA incubator: IWA ≈ 70 mas, ~41 m occulter, ~61,000 km separation, with a coherent optical and mission-geometry budget.

Lunar optical-communications smallsat (SPENVIS TID bounding, propulsion sizing, laser-comm CONOPS); interstellar exoplanet-atmosphere probe payload trades; sub-GEO debris-monitoring constellation — published across IAC 2023–2025.
